The Stem Cell Clinic Evaluation Checklist

12 Questions to Ask
Before You Choose a Provider

A physician-written guide to evaluating any regenerative medicine provider with clarity, before you commit to treatment.

Regenerative medicine has grown quickly, and oversight across providers has not always kept pace. Two clinics offering the same therapy name can differ enormously in the physician's training, where the biologic material comes from, and how honestly outcomes are discussed with you. This checklist gives you twelve questions to bring into any consultation, so you can evaluate a provider with clarity before making a treatment decision, and it draws directly on Dr. Joy Kong's own standards as a triple board-certified physician in regenerative and longevity medicine.

Bring these twelve questions to any consultation. A provider worth choosing will welcome every one of them.

01 Safety & Regulation

1

Is the clinic using FDA-regulated products, and can they show documentation?

Regenerative medicine is an evolving field, and oversight varies widely between providers. A reputable clinic will document where its products fall within FDA guidelines and answer this question directly, without hesitation.

2

Where do the cells or biologics come from, and how are they processed and stored?

Sourcing and handling directly affect safety and consistency. Ask where the material originates, how it is processed, and under what conditions it is stored before use.

3

Is the procedure performed in a licensed medical facility, not a spa or wellness center?

Regenerative therapies are medical procedures. They should be performed in a properly licensed clinical setting, with the equipment and protocols a medical procedure requires.

02 Physician Credentials & Experience

4

What credentials does the physician administering treatment hold, and are they board-certified in a relevant specialty?

Ask specifically who will be performing your procedure and confirm their board certifications directly. Marketing materials can feature one physician while a different team member performs the actual treatment.

5

How many procedures like mine has this physician personally performed?

Experience with your specific concern, whether joint, cognitive, or systemic, matters more than years in practice alone. A confident physician will answer this plainly.

6

Is a physician present for the full procedure, or is it delegated to unsupervised staff?

In some clinics, a physician consults but a technician performs the treatment. Know who is actually in the room before you commit.

03 Personalization & Protocol

7

Are treatment protocols individualized to my health history and goals, or is this a one-size-fits-all approach?

Age, health history, and the concern being treated should all shape the protocol recommended to you. Be cautious of any clinic that proposes the same treatment before reviewing your history in depth.

8

What does the intake and diagnostic process look like before treatment is recommended?

A thorough intake, including labs or imaging where appropriate, should come before any treatment plan. If a plan is proposed before your history is fully reviewed, that is worth questioning.

9

What realistic outcomes can I expect, and how are they communicated?

Ask whether expected outcomes are shared in writing, supported by data, or only described verbally in general terms. Responsible providers are specific about what regenerative medicine can and cannot do for your situation.

04 Cost & Transparency

10

Is pricing transparent and provided in writing before I commit?

You should receive a clear, itemized cost estimate before agreeing to treatment, not a verbal figure that changes once you are in the office.

11

What is included in that price, and what could add cost later?

Ask directly whether follow-up visits, additional sessions, or supporting therapies are included, or whether they will be billed separately as treatment progresses.

12

Does the clinic pressure same-day decisions, or is there room to think it over?

A trustworthy provider welcomes questions and gives you time to decide. High-pressure, same-day commitment tactics are a signal to slow down, regardless of how promising the treatment sounds.
